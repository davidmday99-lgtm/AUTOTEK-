TEXA Software Update June 2026: What's New for Technicians

By Mike Miller

TEXA has rolled out its June 2026 IDC5 software update, and this one brings significant improvements for shops working on the latest vehicles. Here's what technicians need to know about the new features and expanded coverage.

Electric Vehicle Diagnostics: The update adds comprehensive support for 2026 model year EVs from Ford, GM, and Hyundai/Kia. This includes high-voltage battery state of health testing, thermal management system diagnostics, and charging system fault isolation. With EV market share continuing to grow, these capabilities are becoming essential for any full-service shop.

Enhanced Battery Testing: For conventional vehicles, the update improves 12V battery and charging system analysis. New algorithms provide more accurate state-of-health predictions, helping technicians identify batteries that are likely to fail before they leave customers stranded. This is especially valuable heading into summer, when heat accelerates battery degradation.

Expanded OEM Functions: The June update unlocks additional service functions for European makes including Mercedes-Benz, BMW, and Audi. Technicians can now perform certain module programming tasks, service resets, and component adaptations that previously required dealer-level tools.

If you haven't updated your TEXA diagnostic system recently, now is the time. The June 2026 update ensures you're ready to service the newest vehicles rolling into your bays. Contact ProAutoTek for assistance with updates or to learn more about expanding your diagnostic capabilities.
