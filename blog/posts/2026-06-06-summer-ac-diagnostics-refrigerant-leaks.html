Summer A/C Diagnostics: Finding Refrigerant Leaks Before the Heat Wave

By Mike Miller

With temperatures across the country already pushing into the 90s this first week of June, shops are seeing a surge in A/C complaints. Customers describe everything from "warm air only" to "cooling works sometimes" to that dreaded musty smell. For technicians, the challenge is quickly pinpointing whether the issue is low refrigerant, a failing compressor, or something in between.

Modern diagnostic tools have transformed A/C troubleshooting. Rather than guessing or relying solely on pressure gauges, today's scan tools can read A/C system data directly from the vehicle's climate control module. You can monitor compressor clutch engagement, refrigerant pressure sensor readings, blend door positions, and evaporator temperature in real time.

When refrigerant is low, the next question is always: where's it going? TEXA diagnostic systems can help isolate the problem by identifying which components are reporting abnormal values. Combined with a UV dye test or electronic leak detector, you can find that slow leak at the condenser, evaporator, or line fitting much faster than traditional methods.

For shops preparing for the summer rush, now is the time to verify your A/C diagnostic capabilities are up to date. The latest TEXA software updates include expanded coverage for 2024-2026 model year vehicles with updated R-1234yf systems and dual-zone climate controls. Don't let outdated software slow you down when the heat is on.
