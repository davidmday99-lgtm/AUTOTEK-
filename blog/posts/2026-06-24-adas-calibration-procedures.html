ADAS Calibration Procedures

June 24, 2026 · 9 min read · Advanced Systems

Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) are now standard equipment on most new vehicles. After windshield replacement, collision repair, or sensor service, proper calibration is critical for safety. Let's walk through what you need to know to become ADAS-competent.

Why ADAS Calibration Matters

ADAS systems depend on precise sensor alignment and calibration. A misaligned camera or radar sensor can mean:

  • Collision avoidance systems not activating when needed
  • Lane keep assist drifting into adjacent lanes
  • Adaptive cruise control maintaining wrong distances
  • Liability and safety risks for your shop and customer
⚠️ Critical: In many states, shops are now required to recalibrate ADAS after certain repairs. Check your local regulations and manufacturer requirements.

Types of ADAS Calibration

There are two primary calibration methods:

Static Calibration

Performed in your shop without driving the vehicle:

  • Equipment needed: Specialized calibration mirrors, targets, and alignment fixtures
  • Time required: 30 minutes to 2 hours depending on system
  • Cameras: Most commonly calibrated using mirror targets and software
  • Radar: Many vehicles now require front radar static recalibration after service
  • Procedure: Position vehicle at precise distance from target(s), run scan tool calibration routine
💡 Tip: Static calibration requires a very clean environment and level floor. Temperature matters too—let sensors acclimate before starting.

Dynamic Calibration

Performed by driving the vehicle on the road:

  • Equipment needed: Scan tool and calibration software only
  • Time required: 20-50 miles of highway driving under specific conditions
  • Conditions: Usually requires clear weather, well-marked lane lines, speeds between 35-65 mph
  • Best for: Lane keep assist, 360-degree cameras, some radar systems
  • Advantage: Real-world conditions provide accurate alignment

Common ADAS Sensor Types

Different sensors require different approaches:

  • Forward-facing cameras: Used for lane departure, collision avoidance, traffic sign recognition
  • Radar sensors: Adaptive cruise control, collision prevention—frequency varies by manufacturer
  • LiDAR: Luxury vehicles use LiDAR for precise object detection—newer systems still being standardized
  • 360-degree cameras: Surround-view parking assistance—requires multi-point calibration

Step-by-Step Calibration Process

General flow for static camera calibration:

  1. Park vehicle on level floor with engine off
  2. Connect scan tool and enter ADAS calibration mode
  3. Position target mirror at distance specified by vehicle software (typically 8-15 feet)
  4. Ensure camera has clear, unobstructed view of target
  5. Initiate calibration routine—camera adjusts its alignment
  6. Verify calibration successful via scan tool confirmation message
  7. Perform safety check drive (typically 20-25 mph test) if system requires it

Equipment Investment

Setting up ADAS capability in your shop requires upfront investment:

  • Basic system: $2,000-5,000 (mirror targets, basic software)
  • Comprehensive system: $8,000-15,000 (multiple targets for radar, all camera types)
  • Premium system: $20,000+ (LiDAR capable, integrated with alignment equipment)
🔧 Pro tip: ADAS calibration is a revenue opportunity. Shops can charge $200-500 per calibration. With increasing ADAS adoption, this pays for equipment quickly.

Common Calibration Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls:

  • Skipping calibration after windshield replacement—it's likely required
  • Calibrating in poor lighting or dirty environments—affects accuracy
  • Not following vehicle manufacturer-specific procedures—specs vary widely
  • Forgetting to clear ADAS-related DTCs before calibration

Training and Certification

Consider upskilling your team:

  • Most equipment manufacturers provide online certification courses
  • ASE offers ADAS certification through their collision repair pathway
  • Dealer-specific training available through manufacturer service portals
