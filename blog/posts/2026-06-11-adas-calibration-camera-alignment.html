ADAS Calibration: Camera Alignment and Procedure

By Mike Miller

Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) have become standard safety equipment. When a vehicle goes through collision repair or windshield replacement, camera calibration is critical. Misaligned cameras compromise safety features and create serious liability issues for your shop. Proper calibration is no longer optional—it's an essential service that modern shops must offer.

Why Calibration Matters: Forward-facing cameras mounted in windshields detect lane markings, pedestrians, and vehicles ahead. If the camera shifts even a few millimeters during windshield replacement or collision repair, lane keeping assistance, automatic emergency braking, and adaptive cruise control become unreliable or inoperate. A customer could miss a critical safety alert because your shop didn't calibrate the system. This creates obvious liability exposure.

Static vs. Dynamic Calibration: Static calibration uses alignment targets and is performed in a controlled bay using proper fixtures and reference points. Most cameras can be statically calibrated using specialized equipment and scan tools. Dynamic calibration requires road testing to verify the system performs correctly under real-world conditions. Many modern vehicles require both static calibration followed by dynamic verification.

Using Diagnostic Tools for Calibration: TEXA and competing platforms provide ADAS calibration functions integrated into their software. After windshield replacement or repair, connect your diagnostic tool and access the ADAS calibration module. Follow OEM procedures for your specific model—different manufacturers have different requirements. Some require specific targets; others use the vehicle's natural environment for calibration. Always refer to the OEM repair manual for exact procedures.

Documentation and Records: Keep detailed records of every ADAS calibration performed. Document the vehicle VIN, date, mileage, which systems were calibrated, and which test procedures were completed. This documentation protects your shop in case of future claims and demonstrates due diligence to insurance adjusters and customers.

Training and Investment: Invest in proper ADAS training for your technicians. The cost of calibration equipment and training is offset quickly by the service revenue and liability protection. Don't let windshield shops or body shops handle ADAS calibration without proper equipment—your reputation and customer safety depend on it.
