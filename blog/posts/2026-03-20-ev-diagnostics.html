Electric Vehicle Diagnostics: What Shops Need to Know Now

By Mike Miller

Electric vehicles are no longer a niche market. With EV sales growing every year and more models hitting the road from every manufacturer, shops that can diagnose and service EVs have a significant competitive advantage. Here's what you need to know to get started.

EV Diagnostics Are Different

EVs still have an OBD-II port, but the critical systems — battery management, motor controllers, charging systems, thermal management — require specialized diagnostic capabilities. A standard code reader won't cut it. You need a tool that can communicate with EV-specific modules and interpret the data correctly.

Safety First

Working on high-voltage systems requires proper training and equipment. The battery packs in modern EVs operate at 400-800 volts. Before any diagnostic work, technicians need to understand isolation procedures, wear appropriate PPE, and know how to safely de-energize the system. This isn't optional — it's life-or-death.

The Service Opportunity

EVs don't need oil changes, but they do need service. Battery health checks, brake system inspections (regenerative braking changes wear patterns), cooling system service, tire rotations (EVs are heavy and eat tires), and charging system diagnostics are all recurring service opportunities.

Charging System Diagnostics

One of the fastest-growing service categories is charging system diagnostics. When an EV won't charge or charges slowly, the problem could be in the onboard charger, the charge port, the battery management system, or the thermal management system. Shops that can quickly diagnose charging issues will capture this growing market.

Getting Ready

The best time to prepare for EV service was yesterday. The second-best time is today. Invest in EV diagnostic capabilities, get your team trained, and start marketing your EV services. The vehicles are already on the road — the question is whether your shop is ready to service them.
