Electrical Diagnostics: Preparing for Summer Heat

By Mike Miller

Summer heat accelerates electrical failure. Batteries that are borderline in spring will die in July. Alternators strain to meet increased AC load demands. Wiring connections become brittle. This is when your electrical diagnostic skills pay off—before customers get stranded on the highway.

Battery State of Health Testing: Modern diagnostic tools can measure cold cranking amps (CCA), voltage drop under load, and conductance. The key is testing batteries before summer rather than after failure. A battery reading 80% CCA in June will likely fail by August. Proactive replacement prevents emergency roadside calls and builds customer trust.

Alternator Output and Ripple Voltage: Heat reduces alternator lifespan. Measure alternator output (should be 13.5-14.8V) and ripple voltage (should be under 0.5V). Excessive ripple indicates rectifier diodes beginning to fail. Catching this early means a planned alternator replacement, not a stranded customer.

Wiring and Connector Analysis: High heat makes corroded connections worse. Use your multimeter to measure voltage drops across connections. A 0.5V drop at a battery terminal connection indicates serious corrosion that must be cleaned or replaced. Pay special attention to ground straps and engine block grounds.

Air Conditioning Load Testing: AC compressor engagement loads the electrical system significantly. Watch how voltage changes when AC engages. If voltage drops below 12.5V with AC running, the charging system is marginal. This is especially critical in summer when AC runs constantly.

Invest time in thorough electrical diagnostics now. Your shop's reputation and customer satisfaction will improve dramatically when fewer vehicles break down during the hottest weeks of the year.
